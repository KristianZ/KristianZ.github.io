Polish Scouting Organization

As a preschooler, my parents enrolled me into the Polish Scouting Organization. Little did I know that in addition to the fun games, I would mentor inner-city Chicago teens to be better leaders. Alongside teaching scouting ideals and preserving Polish heritage. I would network and lead scouts from all corners of the globe. I would explore nature and learn about my planet. I'm incredibly thankful for my experiences in this organization, and I'm always wishing to give back more.

As a troop leader from 2014 to 2017, I conducted several major kayaking and winter trips per year. These trips surrounded teens in nature without technology. Trip topics taught Polish heritage, leadership, and independence. In 2015, I assisted in running the Chicago camp at the United States jamboree. In 2017, I backpacked with scouts from around the world at the global jamboree in Canada. Most recently in 2018, I organized a two week trip around Lake Michigan for the Chicago scouts.

I created a short video that highlights my 2018 trip. For my earlier experiences, a friend created several videos. Embedded are my three favorite videos.

White and Red Ball. Volunteer dancing!

The Legion of Young Polish Women hosts a ball at the end of every winter. The Queen of the ball is awarded to the dubutante that fundraises the most money prior to the ball. For several years now, I've volunteered as a mazur dancer. Mazur dancers dance with former dubutantes as a celebration after the Queen is crowned. For me, this is an enjoyable volunteer event that allows me to support women and my Polish heritage.

Tutoring. We learn together.

As an undergraduate student at Rose-Hulman, I am a member of Eta Kappa Nu, an IEEE honors society for electrical and computer engineers. Alongisde my academics, I dedicate several hours a week after classes to guide students through homework and projects. Ultimately, I hope to continue the society's tradition by building ethical and technical leaders in the industry.
