Track and Field. The life of a multi-event athlete.

I spend a lot of time practicing the sport of Track and Field. While I'm a sprinter and pole vaulter first, I'm currently training for the decathlon. Understandably, track and field meets are long and many have lost interest in the sport over time. However, my first-hand experience has led me to appreciate the many physical feats. This has made me love the sport so much more.

I've never had an opportunity to share my track accomplishments, but I'm really proud of them and wish to share them somewhere. My high school competed in Illinois 3A track. I was a member of our state-qualifying 4x200m and 4x100m relays. I was also a conference scorer in the pole vault, 55m dash, 4x100m, and 4x200m. My college was DIII. I was a member of our conference-winning 4x200m and 4x400m relays, a three-time conference scorer in the pole vault, and a conference scorer in the 400m hurdles.

Study Abroad. Studying in Rome, Italy!

One of the best and most adventerous 5 weeks of my life were in Italy. In the summer of 2018, I chose to take a sociology course and a podcasting and vidcasting course in Rome, Italy. Every week, my sociology course would explore neighborhoods of Rome and discuss their history, culture, and identity. My podcasting and vidcasting course would create one or two podcasts and vidcasts every week.

On weekends, my friends and I would have fun exploring the country. Of course, I visited the Vatican several times. I also travelled to Ostia, Venice, Florence, Cinque Terre, Naples, Pompei, Sorrento, and Capri. I even had the opportunity to see a Killers concert! I've never had a better opportunity to connect with new people and explore a historically and culturally rich country.
